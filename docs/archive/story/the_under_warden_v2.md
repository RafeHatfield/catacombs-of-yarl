# The Under-Warden — second pass

*Filed off, expanded, and significantly improved*

---

## What changed and why

Three structural updates from the first draft, plus a half-dozen smaller tunings.

**The wand IS the familiar.** Your idea reframes the whole proposal in the right direction. Instead of a creature in an amulet who happens to be present alongside the wand of portals, the wand *itself* is conscious — a single signature item Sasha has carried for years, who talks in his head, who casts the portals through Sasha's hand, who has opinions, who has a name. This collapses two design slots into one and makes the relationship more intimate. **You don't have a familiar AND a wand. You have a wand who is your familiar, and she's the most important object you own.** Spellbreaker's register meets Loiosh's voice in a single brass-and-bone instrument. Genuinely better than the original. Thank you.

**The orc faction is fully built out.** "Tier-1 humanoid threat" was the laziest part of the first draft. The Unshriven are now a full society with castes, internal politics, an active war with the deeper undead, and a specific reason they exist — they're soldiers who died wrong, reincarnated into orc-bodies as a punishment-judgment from the Under-Warden's predecessor, bound to the upper Paths and forever fighting for the boundary they can't cross. They get a king, a war-cult, a drunk diplomat, a recurring character or two, and tactical interactions specific to them.

**Brust references filed off, but the love letter is intact.** No jhereg, no Verra, no Greymist, no Eastern-vs-Dragaeran framing. The PC stays a swarthy professional from a culture viewed as "lesser" by the deeper afterlife. The patron stays a patron — but she's named for what she does, not borrowed. The voice stays Vlad-adjacent because that's what you wanted, and voice isn't copyrightable. **What we lose:** specific recognition by Brust readers. **What we keep:** the entire register that made it work. Net: small loss, large protection.

Other smaller changes flagged inline.

## A) One-breath pitch

**You're a retired killer who descended into the Paths of the Dead to buy back a soul you owe a debt to, carrying a brass wand that talks in your head and opens portals through your hand — and the dungeon wants to weigh you.** Twenty-five floors of mobile turn-based roguelike that plays exactly like Shattered Pixel Dungeon for the first ninety seconds so your muscle memory works, then quietly becomes smarter. Every monster from the Oryx tileset is present and recognizable — orcs, undead, clerics, monsters, all four factions — but each has one specific tactical interaction the game never tells you about, and the person telling you about it is the wand. You can wear an enemy's body if you're willing to leave yours bleeding on the floor. The signature item is a single conscious wand named **Hollowmark** — she does portals, she breaks spells, and she will not shut up. **The Under-Warden** is the title because the thing in charge of the Paths has a job title, and by the end of the run you will have opinions about him.

## B) The PC — Sasha of Reven, retired, owes the Lady One

**Name:** Sasha of Reven. Forty-two. Small, dark-haired, walks with a favored right knee from a job that went wrong six years ago. Retired four years. Retirement did not take.

**Profession:** What used to be called a *specialist*. He killed people for money. He's not proud of it, but he's not ashamed in the way the priests would prefer either. He was good. He stopped because the work stopped feeling like work and started feeling like the only thing he knew how to do, which scared him. He lives now in a small house above a vineyard he cannot keep up with. He owes back-rent. The vineyard is patient. His landlord, a stout woman named Tova who used to be a Watch sergeant, is less patient but is not unkind.

**Culture:** Sasha is **Revenese** — from the south coast, dark-skinned, considered foreign by the highland kingdoms whose afterlife the Paths technically belong to. The Revenese have their own gods, their own death-rites, their own opinions about whether the Paths even apply to them. This is the load-bearing detail that was previously "Eastern" — it gives Sasha standing to refuse the Under-Warden's claims and gives the Under-Warden his procedural grievance. Cultural friction without proper-noun theft.

**Patron:** **The Lady of the Long Hour**. A southern Revenese goddess, smaller than the highland pantheon, more practical. She accepts contracts. She closes them. She does not love her servants — she is fair to them. Sasha has known her for twenty years. He owes her a soul: **Anik's**, a woman who died because Sasha took a contract he should have refused. The Lady offered him a trade — descend the Paths, find Anik before she reaches the Hall of Judgment, bring her back. In exchange, his ledger with the Lady closes.

He knows the Lady is not telling him the whole truth. He went anyway, because he owes her, and because Anik mattered.

**Voice register:** The same target as before — pragmatic, dry, fear-acknowledged-sideways, gear-treated-affectionately, debts-tracked. Brust-adjacent but not Brust-derivative. **He uses the word "work" where another PC would say "combat."** **He never glorifies a fight.** **He calls Hollowmark "the wand" in public, by name in private, and "Holl" only when stressed.** This last detail matters. It tells the player who the wand is to him without ever stating it.

**Five diary samples** (these set the tone for every text fragment in the game):

> **Entry 1, Floor 1.** *The stairs don't end; they just stop pretending to. Holl says this is a bad idea. Noted. Took the first step anyway. The air smells like old coins. Down we go.*

> **Entry 4, Floor 3, after a near miss.** *Bigger than I like. The thing swung, I wasn't there, I was, I wasn't, and then it was on the floor and I was mostly upright. Had to be done. Need new boots. These have troll on them.*

> **Entry 7, Floor 6, after a bargain.** *She wanted her bones carried one floor up and set in the right alcove. I said yes. She said her name in a language I didn't know and gave me a key. One debt paid, one owed, call it even. I hate this place a little less.*

> **Entry 11, Floor 10, after finding the ring.** *Picked up a ring. Shouldn't have. The finger went cold, then the hand, then something in my chest took an interest. The wand buzzed, which was comforting; she swore, which was less so. Kept the ring. Going to regret it. Aware of this.*

> **Entry 19, Floor 17, after possession.** *Wore an orc for four turns. Worst thing I've ever done, by which I mean the fourth or fifth. You forget your own arms have the reach you're used to. When I climbed out he looked at me like he knew. Then I stabbed him with his own cleaver. Fair's fair.*

The diary remains the only narrative delivery vehicle outside item flavor. One short entry per floor entered, auto-composed from a pool that reacts to what just happened on the previous floor. ~25-30 entries per run from a pool of ~150, eight to forty words each. Sasha is telling you this story afterward, which is why he gets to foreshadow and undercut himself.

## C) Hollowmark — the wand who is the familiar

**This is the proposal's biggest single change, and it's a major upgrade.** The wand of portals is no longer a tool with a separate companion — it IS the companion. One object, one voice, one signature item.

**Name:** Hollowmark. Sasha calls her Holl. The wand, in turn, calls Sasha "Boss" — same as the original familiar — because she did, once, when he saved her from a fence forty years ago, and she has never quite let it go.

**Form:** A brass-and-bone wand the length of a forearm, with a single smoky quartz set in the grip and three concentric brass rings around the shaft that rotate when she's thinking. She fits in a leather sheath at Sasha's hip. She does not have eyes. She has, on inspection, a single small mark burned into the brass near the cap — a hollow circle with a line through it. Hence the name.

**What she is:** A *wand of conscience*, an exceptionally rare class of magical instrument from before the kingdom's founding. The lore (drip-fed via item flavor and Hollowmark's own stories) is that the wand-of-conscience tradition was a cooperative one between mage and instrument — the wand carries the soul of a previous wielder, who agreed at death to be bound into the brass for one further span of service. Hollowmark's previous wielder was a woman named Marya Hollowmark, a southern artificer who specialized in spatial magic. She died of plague at thirty-seven. She agreed to be bound. She is the wand. **The wand has a person's worth of memory, opinion, and competence behind every line she speaks**, which is why she sounds the way she does.

**What she does, mechanically:** Three things, all native to the existing engine.

1. **Portals.** Per the dev's existing mechanic — two portal markers, infinite charges, third use collapses the pair and resets. Hollowmark casts these through Sasha's hand. He aims; she opens.

2. **Spell-breaking** (your suggestion — kept, integrated). Once per floor, Hollowmark can **dispel one active magical effect** within 5 tiles. Cursed item locked to a body part. A summoning circle being prepared by a cleric. A poison cloud sustained by a spellcaster. A polymorph effect on Sasha himself. She picks; you trigger. The cooldown resets per floor descent, no exceptions.

3. **Identification, slow.** Hollowmark identifies one unidentified item per **three floors** by running her smoky quartz over it overnight. (Diegetic: she takes a long, hot bath in the item's magic.) The player chooses which item from their inventory each time. Not a tutorial, not a hint engine — a tool with a real cost (you can only identify ~8 items per run this way) that frees the player from the worst NetHack price-ID grind without trivializing the identification minigame.

**Voice:** Loiosh's register intact, transferred to a brass instrument. Cynical. Loyal. Tactically sharp. Self-deprecating about her own existence. Calls Sasha "Boss." Never explains a mechanic Sasha has already used. Has her own jokes about being a wand ("*at least I don't get hangovers*" / "*I haven't had to pee in forty years, which I count as a win*"). Has memories of her previous life as Marya — these surface rarely, never on demand, but they're load-bearing for the late game.

**Delivery mechanism:** An **italic-purple text ribbon** at the bottom of the screen, persisting for four seconds per line, max one line on screen at a time. A small wand-tip-glow icon in the HUD pulses when she speaks. No voice acting required. Localizable. Minimal UI footprint. **The same delivery mechanism as the previous draft, just with a wand icon instead of an amulet icon.**

**Silence rules** (non-negotiable, defuses the Navi problem):
- **Every line is authored once per run.** Pool-based. No repetition. If a trigger fires a second time in a run, a different pool member fires, or nothing.
- **Combat silence by default.** During active combat (any enemy in sight and within 3 tiles), Hollowmark only speaks if HP crosses 25%, 10%, 1%, or if a new monster species appears.
- **Idle only after 20+ silent turns.** She volunteers comments only when the player stops engaging.
- **The player can tell her to shut up.** A UI button on the wand-icon: tap → muted for the rest of the run. She has a single acknowledgment: *"Right. Fine. Call me when something eats you."* Tap again to un-mute. Players who find her charming will never touch it. Players who don't, will, and will feel respected.
- **Verbose / Tactical-only / Silent** settings toggle in options.

**Trigger taxonomy** (~1,200 lines authored across all triggers and pools, three runs to fully cycle through):

| Trigger | Lines per run | Example |
|---|---|---|
| First sighting of new monster species | ~45 (one per species) | *"That's a Hall Warden. He's going to write you up. Hit fast."* |
| First entry to a new region | 5 | *"This is the Boundary. The Unshriven hold it because they have to. We pass through because we have to. Try not to make friends."* |
| HP at 25% | 1 per run | *"Boss. You're leaking."* |
| HP at 10% | 1 per run | *"Boss. BOSS. Drink something."* |
| HP at 1% | 1 per run | *"Goodbye."* |
| Trap triggered (first of each type) | ~8 across game | *"Acid. On purpose, I hope."* |
| Item identified | pool of ~40 | *"Ah. It's that kind of potion. Good."* |
| Hollowmark identifies an item overnight | pool of ~50 | *"Slept on it. It's a Scroll of Fire. Not the most subtle option, but it's yours now."* |
| Spell-break used | pool of ~12 | *"Done. Whatever that priest was building, he's started over. He looks unhappy. Good."* |
| Kill streak ≥5 no-damage | 3 per run | *"Showing off, are we."* |
| Long idle | 3 per run | *"Any decade now, Boss."* |
| On death | 1 reactive per cause | *"Poisoned. By a trap you set off on purpose. Subtle."* |
| On possession enter | 1 per species (~25 lines) | *"Ugh. This one smells like wet dog."* |
| On home body drain starting | 1 per run | *"They're eyeing your body, Boss."* |
| On possession exit successful | ~6 across game | *"See. I knew you'd make it. I lied, but I knew."* |
| Marya-memory surfaces (rare) | ~12 across game, each one-shot | *"The artificer who taught me this trick had a cat. The cat hated me. I miss it."* |
| Between runs (results screen) | ~50 reactive lines | *"Twenty-two floors. Not bad. Not good. Somewhere in between, which is where you live, I guess."* |

**Tactical intel delivery:** Hollowmark sees through the portals she opens. **When you place a portal, she scouts the far side**: *"Three shades and a Warden on the other side. Don't walk through."* This is the single best mechanical-narrative integration in the design. The signature item, the signature voice, and the signature tactic are all one thing.

**Why this is better than the amulet-creature original:** the amulet+creature split asked the player to keep two pieces of magical equipment in mind. The wand-as-familiar is *one* item, the most important one, that does everything. It's a stronger story (a wand who was once a person), a stronger UI (one icon), and a stronger thematic anchor (your most-used object is your closest companion). Every time the player taps the wand to open a portal, they're consulting Holl. Every time they cast a spell-break, that's Holl saving them. Every time she identifies an item overnight, it's a small intimate scene. **The bond is built into the verbs.**

**What the player learns about Marya, slowly, across runs:** She was a southern artificer. She specialized in spatial magic — portals, dimensional folds, summoning. She lived in a town called **Wend**, which the highland kingdoms erased in a war seventy years before Sasha was born. She had a sister named Ana who survived and went north. Ana's grandchildren are alive somewhere in the highland kingdoms. Marya doesn't know. Hollowmark suspects. **The information surfaces in Marya-memory triggers across multiple runs, never on demand, never as a quest.** A player who plays five runs gets fragments. A player who plays twenty gets the picture. A player who plays fifty knows Hollowmark better than Sasha does.

This is the metaprogression-of-voice the original proposal had with the familiar, now anchored to a real character with a real backstory who is *also* the signature gameplay item. Massive upgrade.

## D) The antagonist — the Under-Warden, who is still a clerk

Largely unchanged from the first draft. Quick recap:

**The Under-Warden** is a bureaucrat of the dead. He inherited management of the Paths centuries ago when the gods who ran them lost interest, and has been doing the job competently and joylessly ever since. He wears the robes of a senior cleric, carries a ledger, speaks like someone counting inventory. His problem with Sasha is procedural: Sasha is **Revenese**, the Paths' charter does not technically cover Revenese, and the Under-Warden hates technicalities because technicalities make paperwork.

He sends memos. Polite at first. They grow less polite.

The boss fight on floor 25 is him not because he's the strongest thing in the Paths (he isn't) but because he holds the ledger that holds Anik's line. You don't kill him. You **audit** him.

His voice in three sample memos:

> **Memo 7, Floor 9.** *Specialist of Reven — your continued descent has been logged. The Charter of the Paths (Article 4, Section 12) provides for unauthorized entrants to be referred to the Hall of Judgment for processing. I have made the referral. There is no need to reply. — UW*

> **Memo 14, Floor 17.** *Specialist — the volume of your unauthorized soul-transfers (please find attached: 6 unshriven, 2 wardens, 1 honored dead) has now exceeded the threshold for procedural notice and entered the territory of formal complaint. I am required by the Charter to log a formal complaint. The complaint is logged. There is, again, no need to reply. — UW*

> **Memo 22, Floor 24.** *Specialist Sasha — I am told by the Weigher that you have collected three Stones of Intuition. I am told by the Hall that you have spoken with one of the freed Honored. I am told by the Boundary that the Unshriven King has acknowledged you. None of this is technically permitted. All of it is technically possible. I will see you on twenty-five. Please do not be late. — UW*

The Under-Warden is not malicious. He is tired. He is doing his job. The fact that his job involves preventing Sasha from saving someone is not, to him, personal. **This makes him the right kind of villain for a love-letter roguelike** — he's the rules made flesh, not a cackling demon. His grief, discoverable in a side area on floor 24, is that he was once a man who loved a woman who died, and he took the job because it meant he could see her sometimes when she came through the Hall. She passed through eighty years ago. He hasn't seen her since. He has not asked the Hall what happened to her, because asking would mean admitting he's been hoping. He just keeps doing the work.

You can offer him Hollowmark on floor 25 in exchange for Anik. He will refuse. **You cannot solve him.** He is just a clerk who lost someone and built a life around the loss. This is the love-letter version of the dragon-widower from your original story, scaled down to a side character — a quiet acknowledgment of where you started.

## D-prime) The orcs — the Unshriven, a full caste society

**This is the section that needed the most rebuilding from the first draft.** Here it is, properly.

**Origin lore:** When the Under-Warden's predecessor, **First-Warden Mathuren the Just**, took office four hundred years ago, she inherited a problem: the upper Paths were full of soldiers who had died wrong. Battle-deaths without rites. Deserters murdered by their own. Mercenaries who outlived their contracts and were buried in unmarked ground. The Paths' original judgment system did not handle these well — the souls were neither blessed nor cursed enough to sort. They piled up. They became a riot.

Mathuren's solution was the **Reincorporation Rite**: she bound the unsorted soldiers into orc-bodies — a hardier form, capable of long service in the upper Paths — and gave them a job. **Hold the Boundary against the deeper undead, who would otherwise climb up and overrun the surface tombs.** The Unshriven were not exiles or punishments in their own self-conception. They were *posted*. They had a duty. The duty was eternal. Mathuren forgot to mention the eternal part.

Four hundred years later, the Unshriven are a society. They have:

- **A king,** **King Borrek the Long-Posted**, who has held the Boundary for two hundred years and is tired in a specific way that no living mortal can quite understand. He holds court on **Floor 4** in a hall built into the cliff above the river that marks the upper Boundary. He is old, scarred, tactically brilliant, and *bored*.

- **Three castes**, originally military hierarchies that hardened into something more like genuine class distinctions over centuries:
  - **Bone-Orcs** (former officers, command class, ~20% of population): scarred, intelligent, weapon-discipline tier. The captains, the strategists. Most of the named orc characters come from this caste.
  - **Iron-Orcs** (former rank-and-file infantry, ~60%): the workhorses. Spear-and-shield squads. Most of the orcs the player fights are Iron-Orcs.
  - **Hollow-Orcs** (former deserters and mercenary leftovers, ~20%): smaller, sneakier, knife-fighters. The Unshriven officially treat them as untouchables; the Hollow-Orcs run the smuggling operations that keep the orc economy alive.

- **A war-cult**, the **Cult of the Boundary**, devoted to the holy duty of keeping the deeper undead from rising. They burn bone-incense at the river-altar daily. They are devout. Their devotion is, increasingly, performance — most of them privately suspect the duty is a punishment, but admitting this would unravel the only meaning they have.

- **An economy.** They mine the Paths' upper rock for iron and salt. They trade with the few living visitors who descend (mostly Revenese pilgrims, who recognize the orcs as fellow outsiders and treat them well). They drink a fermented mushroom-wine called **borr-lath** that Hollowmark says smells like a regret she hasn't had yet.

- **A diplomatic problem.** Sasha is Revenese. Revenese pilgrims are tolerated. Revenese specialists carrying a wand-of-conscience are *interesting*. The Bone-Orc captains will trade with Sasha. The Iron-Orcs will fight him by default but can be negotiated with at the king's command. The Hollow-Orcs will sell him things no one else has and rob him given an opening.

**Named orc characters** (three, scoped tight):

1. **King Borrek the Long-Posted.** Floor 4. The diplomatic hub of the orc region. Sasha can pay tribute (gold, an interesting item, a Revenese keepsake), receive an audience, and earn the **Boundary-Token** — a chunk of carved bone that, while carried, gives Sasha free passage through any Iron-Orc patrol. Borrek's arc across runs: the first audience he is wary, the third he is curious, the eighth he is openly friendly, the fifteenth he asks Sasha if there is news from the surface that he, Borrek, has been afraid to ask about. Two-hundred-year-old man wanting to know if his daughter's bloodline still holds the family land. (It does. Sasha can tell him. He weeps. He gives Sasha his old battle-knife.)

2. **Captain Vesh the Blade-Drunk.** Floor 6. A Bone-Orc captain, alcoholic, brilliant. Runs the eastern boundary patrols. Will hire Sasha for a one-floor job: clear out a deeper-undead intrusion into the orc cave network. Pays in a Stone of Intuition (load-bearing for the Ledger end-game) and a useful weapon. Across runs, Vesh gets sloppier, more morose, and eventually asks Sasha to bring him a specific Revenese spirit he remembers from his living life. If Sasha can find it (rare drop on floor 8), Vesh pours him one and tells him a story that gives him a permanent +1 to a stat for that run.

3. **Hael the Hollow-Orc Smuggler.** Floor 5, in a hidden alcove behind a waterfall. Sells items the regular shopkeepers don't. Including, sometimes, **a single charge of Holy Oil** (kills any one undead in a 1-tile radius, no save), which is the kind of item that wins a run. Hael is also the only character in the game who knows what's actually under the Paths beneath the deepest floor. He won't tell you. But across runs, he drops hints, and a player who pieces them together unlocks the **Swap** ending (see Endings).

**Tactical interactions specific to orcs** (additions to the master list, not replacements):

- **Iron-Orc patrols can be diverted by tribute.** Drop two pieces of gold in their patrol path; they pause to argue about who picks it up. Costs 2 gold, buys 4 turns of free movement. Cheaper than fighting.
- **Bone-Orc captains will not pursue you across a boundary marker** (small carved stones at certain dungeon junctions). They have orders. Cross the marker, you're safe — until the next captain.
- **Hollow-Orcs are vulnerable to Holy effects** in a way the other castes aren't (their guilt is closer to the surface). One reading of a Scroll of Light blinds an entire Hollow-Orc squad for 6 turns.
- **The orc-undead war is real and exploitable.** On any floor with both factions present, deeper-undead encounters automatically draw nearby Iron-Orc patrols. Ring the alarm bell at any orc shrine and an Iron-Orc reinforcement squad arrives within 8 turns to fight whatever's in the room. Costs you 1 reputation with the orcs (they don't appreciate being summoned for trivial threats), but pays in a free fight where you don't have to participate.

**Faction reputation:** Sasha has a hidden Reputation score with the Unshriven, tracked +/- across actions. Killing Iron-Orcs unprovoked: -1. Helping Captain Vesh: +3. Trading at Hael's: +1. Insulting the Boundary Cult by stepping on their incense bowls: -2. At reputation +10, the Unshriven let Sasha use their war-tunnels to skip a floor. At reputation -10, they actively hunt him on floors 3-6.

**Why the orcs matter to the design:** the first draft had four faction art assets but only really used one of them (the undead) for narrative weight. The expanded Unshriven give the upper third of the dungeon its own complete society to engage with — politics, side quests, faction reputation, a king to respect, a smuggler to deal with, a war to exploit. **This makes floors 1-8 feel like a place, not a tier.** The deeper floors stay grimmer (the Honored Dead and the Hall Wardens are not societies — they're institutions, then individuals). The upper third is *populated*. This is the right shape for a 25-floor descent.

## E) The world — the Paths of the Dead, lightly redrawn

**The ONE weird idea unchanged:** *You didn't mean to be here.* Sasha descended into **Reven Crypt**, his family tomb, looking for the access point the Lady of the Long Hour told him about. Reven Crypt was built on, or into, or *through* a node of the actual Paths. On floor 1 you are in a normal Revenese tomb. On floor 3 you meet your first non-hostile dead. By floor 5 the walls use a different stone. By floor 10 you know.

**The five regions, redrawn to integrate the orc society:**

| Region | Floors | Aesthetic | Resident factions | Boss |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Reven Crypt** | 1-3 | Revenese tomb — warm sandstone, small offering-niches, your family's name carved everywhere | Rats, Hollow-Orc smugglers (small presence), one ghost | **The Warden of Reven** (ancestor-ghost, refuses to let you leave) |
| **The Boundary** | 4-8 | The orc kingdom — cliff-and-river warzone where the Unshriven hold the line | All three orc castes, occasional deeper-undead intrusion, the Boundary Cult | **The Tide-Hunger** (a deeper-undead that broke through, Borrek hires you to kill it) |
| **The Dimhalls** | 9-13 | Stone-tree halls, perpetual half-dark, the boundary between civilization and the deep dead | Oathbreakers, trolls, skeleton archers, first Hall Warden encounters | **The Hollow King** (an Oathbreaker bound by an oath you can resolve) |
| **The Weighing** | 14-19 | Dante-by-way-of-anyone — contrapasso rooms, weighing-chambers, the bureaucratic deep | Honored Dead, Hall Wardens (heavy presence), king bats, liches | **The Weigher of Hearts** |
| **The Inner Court** | 20-25 | The Halls of Judgment — pillars, scales, the dim glow of dead authority | Named elites of every prior type, one Servant of Old Authority (demon analog), the Under-Warden | **The Under-Warden**, who has a ledger |

**Signature setting details, one or two per region:**

- **The House Shrine** (every region): a small altar where you leave an offering and receive a small boon. Identifies one item of the offered tier. Cross-cultural — the Revenese Shrine in Reven Crypt looks different from the orc Boundary Shrine looks different from the Hall Warden's Shrine in the Weighing.
- **The Stele of the Lost Word** (Region 3, replacing the previous draft's "Finger of Doom"): a single tall stone covered in writing in a language no one alive reads. Examining it costs one turn and permanently reveals the location of stairs on every remaining floor. (Diegetic: Hollowmark reads it. Marya knew the language. The wand has just leveraged her dead artificer's education for your benefit.) Hollowmark: *"It says 'turn left.' Across all of these floors. I think it's a very old joke. The down-stair is always northwest if you turn left first. I don't know why. Marya thought it was funny. I think she was wrong, but she's dead, so I let her have it."*
- **The Branch of Passage** (Region 2 or 3, one per run, consumable key): a single-use item that lets you bypass one locked gate, one charmed door, or one boss-room entry condition. Found in an orc shrine, gifted by the Boundary Cult to those who pay sufficient tribute.
- **The Don't-Look-Back Room** (Region 4, rare vault): a hall where the minimap goes dark. Reach the far end without opening the minimap and the room rewards you with a blessed item. Peek and the reward is replaced with a cursed one. The signature classical-myth gimmick — kept, because it's perfect for the genre.
- **The Contrapasso Rooms** (Region 4, rare vaults): dungeon rooms themed to specific ancient sins, each with an environmental effect. A Wind-Room with directional shoves. A Sludge-Room with movement penalties. A Boiling-Floor-Room with damage-per-turn. Each is a vault in the Brogue sense — designed puzzle using general rules.

**The MacGuffin and death-and-reset are unchanged from the first draft:** the Ledger on floor 25 (audit, not steal); permadeath with reactive death-screens; Hollowmark gets the last word on every death. See sections E-F of the original for full text.

## F-G) The tactical interactions — twenty, lightly retuned

The full list from the first draft stands. Three small updates:

- **Interaction #6 (Wand of Portals)** is now **Hollowmark's** core function. Same six taught uses. The familiar-scouts-the-portal-far-side is now native to her, not a separate ability. **Spell-break** (your suggestion) is added as a seventh use, available once per floor: target any active magical effect within 5 tiles, dispel it. This adds one use case to the master list:
  - **(g) Spell-break: cancel a hostile cleric's summoning ritual mid-cast, kill a poison cloud sustained by a caster, dispel a curse from one of your own equipped items, or break a polymorph effect on yourself.** The cooldown is per-floor and never overlaps with portal use, so the player never has to choose between portals and spell-break — they're separate clocks on the same item.

- **Interaction #15 (Identification through possession)** stays, but now competes with Hollowmark's overnight identification for the player's information budget. Possessing a creature gives you that species' full stats forever. Hollowmark's overnight identification gives you one specific item's enchantments. **Both are valuable; neither replaces the other.** Good design tension.

- **Two new orc-specific interactions slot in (orc-tactics from the orc faction section above):** Iron-Orc patrol diversion via dropped gold; Hollow-Orc Holy-effect vulnerability. These bring the master list from 20 to 22, which is fine — discovery cap for one run is ~12, and the additional content spreads across faction-engaged players.

The full master list otherwise stands as written. Reread it whenever you're ready; nothing else needs revising.
