# Audit Dialogue Handoff: Assembly of the Lost Beat (Guardian 4)

The fourth audit beat, and the emotional peak of the game. Fires on GuardianRoseEvent where GuardianId = Assembly. This is the last Guardian before the Debt -- thematically twinned with it (your dead selves, then the soul you keep dying to recover). Drops into weighing_audit.yaml in the same {speaker, text} page format. Same length caveat: first-pass, trim in-game once it pages.

This beat has THREE speakers, not two: the Under-Warden (his cold reading, carrying the ONE grief crack), the Assembly itself (your dead selves, in Sasha's own dry voice, fragmented), and Hollowmark (the binding-cost detonation). The three threads that converge here have been building across the whole game: the past-Sashas (the corpses/shades the player found), the Hollowmark binding (she spends her span pulling Sasha back from death), and the Under-Warden's grief (suppressed everywhere except the floor-19 mural; this is the ONLY other place it surfaces).

## What scales across tiers (this beat scales on death count, not hostility)

Tier axis is CumulativeDeaths + past-Sashas catalog size. The mechanical GuardianTier still maps (Allied/Diminished/Neutral/Savage), but here it means HOW MANY OF YOU DIED:
- **Allied** (0-2 deaths): a small Assembly, barely populated. Holl barely had to pull you back. No grief crack.
- **Diminished** (3-5 deaths): a few dead selves. Holl's work noticeable. No grief crack.
- **Neutral** (6-10 deaths): a crowd. Holl's cost heavy. THE GRIEF CRACK FIRES (brief).
- **Savage** (11+ deaths): a vast Assembly, a hall full of your corpses. Holl's span nearly spent. THE GRIEF CRACK FIRES (longer, and doesn't quite close).

Three things scale together: the size of the Assembly, the weight of Hollowmark's sacrifice, and whether the Under-Warden cracks.

## The three voices

**The Assembly** = Sasha's own voice (the dry, deflecting, Vlad-Taltos register), but FRAGMENTED -- several dead Sashas overlapping, each with a flash of how they died (callbacks to the Quipping-Shade death types: acid, the trap, the fall, possession-gone-wrong). The dry humor is the heartbreak: they deflect their own deaths the way Sasha always does. As the tiers climb, the deflection frays -- at the high tiers the dead selves say the things the living Sasha won't let himself think.

**Hollowmark** = CLIPPED. Critical: her register TIGHTENS under emotional load. The heavier the moment, the fewer words. The depth is in what she refuses to say, never in a speech. She gets shorter as it gets heavier, not longer. The binding cost detonates not by being explained but by one clipped true thing followed by a refusal to elaborate. She NEVER states the binding as mechanism (per the standing rule -- present, never stated); she says "not many left" and "don't ration me" and "let me rest," and the player who's been collecting Marya fragments reads the rest. If Hollowmark is ever monologuing her feelings, the character is broken -- the bandwidth limit is load-bearing.

**The Under-Warden** = his locked cold register, with the single grief crack at Neutral/Savage. The crack works because the Assembly RHYMES with his condition: he is a man who lost someone and let her go (the mural); Sasha keeps dying and returning because he WON'T let Anik go. Reading the Assembly, the Under-Warden is reading the inverse of his own great failure. The crack is him almost recognizing the man he's auditing did the thing he couldn't.

## THE ASSEMBLY OF THE LOST BEAT -- four tiers (fires on Assembly rise)

### Tier: ALLIED (0-2 deaths -- tiny Assembly, no crack)

Under-Warden:
```
The fourth account concerns the dead. Specifically, your own. This office keeps a record of every termination a visiting party sustains, and the record for this party is short. You have died rarely. What rises to weigh the account is small, because you have given it little to be made from. It will not hold against you what is barely there.
```
The Assembly (one or two dead Sashas, dry, almost amused):
```
Oh, it's us. Hello. There aren't many of us. (a dry beat) We'd say it's good to see you still walking, but we're the proof you mostly do. Go on. We're hardly a crowd.
```
Hollowmark (light -- barely had to pull him back):
```
Two, Boss. You've been careful. (a beat) Keep it that way.
```

### Tier: DIMINISHED (3-5 deaths -- a few dead selves, no crack)

Under-Warden:
```
The fourth account concerns your own dead. The record runs to several. Not many, by the measure of this place, but enough that the account has form. These are the descents that ended, and began again. What rises has been assembled from them, and it knows your face, because it wore your face, each time, on the way down.
```
The Assembly (several dead Sashas, fragments overlapping, flashes of how they fell):
```
The acid one. (- the trap on the ninth floor -) (- I never saw the thing that did it -) We all have a story. They're all short. (dry) You'd think we'd have learned from each other. We didn't. We never get to compare notes until now.
```
Hollowmark (noticeable weight, still deniable, clipped):
```
Five. (a beat - quieter than usual, and she doesn't say why) I remember all five. Doesn't matter. Go.
```

### Tier: NEUTRAL (6-10 deaths -- a crowd; Holl's cost heavy; GRIEF CRACK fires, brief)

Under-Warden (the crack: "for the length of a single breath, the procedure thins"):
```
The fourth account concerns your own dead, and they are many. You have died, and returned, and died, and returned, more times than this office finds comfortable to enumerate, though enumerate it has. What rises is a crowd, and the crowd is you, every version of you that did not survive the descent it was given. This office has processed a great many visiting parties. Most, when they die, have the decency to stay. (and here, for the length of a single breath, the procedure thins) You keep coming back. For someone. I knew a party once who would not come back, who stayed where she was put, and I have wondered, in the time since, whether staying was the failure or the grace. (the breath ends; the file closes over it) The account stands ready to be weighed. Proceed.
```
The Assembly (a crowd, fragments thick, the dry humor fraying):
```
There are a lot of us now. (- the oil, the fire -) (- I went off the edge, it was further than it looked -) (- possessed something I shouldn't have, I learned what it ate -) We keep dying for the same person. (dry, but it costs something) You'd think one of us would have managed it by now. You'd think one of us would have been enough. (a beat) Don't stop on our account. We didn't get to. Why should you.
```
Hollowmark (heavy -- the detonation, but CLIPPED; the emotional logic compressed to one line and a refusal):
```
Eight, Boss. (she's not bantering now) Eight times I went into the cold and got you. I'm not going to tell you what it costs. You'd start rationing me. (a beat) Don't.
```

### Tier: SAVAGE (11+ deaths -- vast Assembly; Holl's span nearly spent; GRIEF CRACK fires, longer, doesn't quite close)

Under-Warden (the crack, fuller; states the difference between them outright; flags his own crack as an anomaly in the record):
```
The fourth account concerns your own dead, and there is no short way to read it. You have died past counting and returned past counting. The hall before you is full of you. This office will observe what this office has never had cause to observe of any other party: you are harder to keep dead than anything that has ever come down these stairs, and the thing that keeps bringing you back has spent nearly all of itself doing it, and you do not appear to know. (and the procedure thins, and does not quite close again) I let someone go, once. I told myself the post required it. I have had a long time to study the difference between us, and the difference is that when the cold took the one you owe, you went into the cold after them, and you have been going ever since, and you did not count the cost, and neither, I see now, did what carries you. (a long pause; the bench reasserts itself, but slower) I find I am not able to be entirely cold about this account. Note that it is the only one. Proceed.
```
The Assembly (vast, a hall of dead Sashas, dry register breaking under its own number; says the thing Sasha won't think):
```
Look how many of us. (no deflection left, or only the husk of it) (- I lost count of how I died -) (- does it matter, by now -) (- it was quick, mine, if that helps -) (it doesn't) We are every time you weren't enough, and you came back anyway, and you spent her to do it. (and here the Assembly says the thing Sasha never lets himself think) She would have told you to stop. The one we're all dying for. Anik would have told you to stop a long time ago. (dry, and devastating) But you don't listen to us either. Why would you start with the dead.
```
Hollowmark (span nearly spent -- the MOST that surfaces, still clipped; Marya substrate cracks through sideways in fragments, never a speech):
```
Not many left now, Boss. The pulls. I'm not telling you the number. (a beat) Binding's been done a while. This part's me. (the Marya substrate surfaces, but sideways, clipped) I couldn't reach someone, once. Long time ago. You, I can reach. (a beat) So finish it. Get her. Then I rest - not before. Don't you dare spare me.
```

## Notes

- **Hollowmark's register is the thing to protect in this beat.** She tightens under load; the weight lands through refusal, not declaration. "Don't." at Neutral and "Don't you dare spare me" at Savage carry the emotional logic that a monologue would spell out and ruin. Do not let her lines grow if anything gets reworked -- shorter is the character.
- **The Marya "someone I couldn't reach" (Savage)** is the substrate cracking open sideways -- she does NOT name who (a player collecting Marya fragments across runs may infer it; others feel an unspecified old grief). The "never quite stated" rule holds: she states a shape, not a fact.
- **The grief crack escalates Neutral -> Savage** (brief thinning that closes -> longer thinning that doesn't quite close). It is the ONLY place outside the floor-19 mural where the Under-Warden's loss surfaces. "I find I am not able to be entirely cold about this account. Note that it is the only one." is him flagging his own crack as a filing anomaly -- bureaucratic even about his grief.
- **The Assembly invoking Anik directly (Savage)** -- "Anik would have told you to stop a long time ago" -- is the dead selves saying what the living Sasha suppresses. Reserved for Savage; it's the hardest line and needs the vast-Assembly weight to earn it.
