# Audit Dialogue Handoff: Oathkeeper Beat (Guardian 2)

The second audit beat. Fires on GuardianRoseEvent where GuardianId = Oathkeeper. Written against the locked register from the Warden-beat calibration. Same length caveat as that handoff: first-pass, expect a trim pass once it pages in-game, do not pre-trim.

This beat introduces a THIRD voice register the Warden beat did not need: the orcish cadence. The Oathkeeper is an Unshriven manifestation, so its own lines (the blunt-Guardian half of each two-part beat) carry the Anglo-Saxon, hammered, tusk-mouth cadence from the Borrek/Vesh voice in the style bible -- short hard words (hold, line, faith, salt, dark, broke), declarative hammer-sentences, repetition for weight, no Latinate flow. The Under-Warden's half stays in his locked cold audit register.

## What makes this beat distinct from the Warden beat (for the remaining beats' sake)

The Warden-of-Wardens was a PROCEDURAL sin (you used the institution's officers as tools; the Under-Warden's cold there was institutional protectiveness -- they were HIS officers). The Oathkeeper is a RELATIONAL sin (you broke faith with a living, sovereign faction). The Under-Warden is not personally aggrieved here -- the orcs aren't his people -- so his register shifts to COMPARISON: the Unshriven kept their faith for four hundred years under terms they hated, and you couldn't keep yours for one descent. The recurring move across all four tiers is him measuring Sasha against the orcs' record. This is the "administratively tidy, doing it right" register the bible flags, landing hardest here because the orcs are THE example of doing it right.

At the Savage tier his crack is different from the Warden beat's: not protectiveness leaking, but the institution so genuinely affronted by the breach of faith that it drops its circumlocution for one sentence ("this office will be plain, which it is rarely moved to be").

## Tier axis

Orc faction reputation + unprovoked orc kills. Allied = kept faith (orc champion joins Sasha's side). Diminished = neutral rep, no unprovoked orc kills. Neutral = neutral rep, some unprovoked orc kills. Savage = orc rep Hostile (betrayed them).

The geas surfaces in the Savage line ("the wanting near killed us" + "There is no geas on us. Not here."). Thematic payoff: the living orcs are bound and cannot advance/punish, but the Oathkeeper -- the judgment of their broken faith -- is NOT bound, and can finally act on the betrayal the living ones are constrained from answering.

## THE OATHKEEPER BEAT -- four tiers (fires on Oathkeeper rise)

### Tier: ALLIED (orc rep Allied)

Under-Warden:
```
The second account concerns the Unshriven, and the faith you kept with them. This office observes that the Unshriven keep their own faith with notable consistency. They have held their post on the Boundary for four hundred years, under terms they did not choose and cannot lay down. They are, in the administrative sense, exemplary. You dealt with them honestly. You did not spend their lives where their lives did not need spending. The account is in good standing, and what rises from it rises as a friend, which this office notes is a rarer outcome than the Unshriven's reputation would suggest.
```
The Oathkeeper (orc champion, joins Sasha's side -- orcish register):
```
You kept faith. We felt it, down on the line. The king does not say such things aloud, so I will say it for him: you stood with us, and now I stand with you. Swing well.
```

### Tier: DIMINISHED (orc rep Neutral, zero unprovoked orc kills)

Under-Warden:
```
The second account concerns the Unshriven. You and they were not friends, but you were not false with them. What blood was spilled between you was spilled in fair exchange, each side knowing the terms. The Unshriven hold no grievance they would name. The Oathkeeper rises, but it rises uncertain, because it cannot find in the record the betrayal it was made to answer. It will test you, lightly, and finding little, it will not press.
```
The Oathkeeper (uncertain, grievance thin -- orcish register):
```
I came up looking for an oath you broke. I do not find it. We fought, you and I, but we fought clean. I will not swing hard at a man who never lied to me.
```

### Tier: NEUTRAL (orc rep Neutral, some unprovoked orc kills)

Under-Warden:
```
The second account concerns the Unshriven, and it is a mixed account. You dealt with them when it served you and spent them when it served you, and some of the lives you took were lives that would have let you pass. The Unshriven do not understand this. They are a people who kill for the holding of the line, never for the convenience of it. The blood you spilled past need is on the file, and the Oathkeeper has come to weigh it. It will press. It thinks you should have known better, and this office is inclined to agree.
```
The Oathkeeper (hardened, wasted blood in it -- orcish register):
```
Some of mine you killed because they stood in your way. That is war; I forgive it. Some you killed because killing was the shorter road. That I do not forgive. We held the line four hundred years and never once took the shorter road. You could not hold yours one walk down.
```

### Tier: SAVAGE (orc rep Hostile)

Under-Warden:
```
The second account concerns the Unshriven, and it is an account of broken faith. They held their post for four hundred years. They kept terms they hated, because terms are terms. You came among them, and you took what they offered, and then you turned on them where it suited you, and you spent their lives as though their lives were yours to spend. This office will be plain, which it is rarely moved to be: the Unshriven did everything correctly, for four centuries, and you did not manage it for the length of a single descent. The Oathkeeper rises at its full weight. It is made of every oath you broke, and it has kept count.
```
The Oathkeeper (full weight of broken faith, savage -- orcish register):
```
Four hundred years we held. Four hundred years, and not one of us broke faith, though the wanting near killed us. Then you. You took the king's salt and you turned on his line. You spent us like coin. We are the oath you broke, Sasha of Reven, and we have come up out of the dark to break you back. There is no geas on us. Not here. Not for this.
```

## Notes

- "Took the king's salt" (Savage) = guest-faith idiom (eating a host's salt = accepting protection and owing loyalty); betrayal-of-guest-faith is among the gravest breaches in the iron-age/Anglo-Saxon register the orcs speak in. Intentional.
- The Allied tier pays off the negative-space orc-court diplomacy from the bible: "The king does not say such things aloud, so I will say it for him." The champion speaks the loyalty Borrek structurally never could. Lands hardest for a player who did the orc questline to Allied.
- "There is no geas on us. Not here. Not for this." (Savage) is the geas payoff -- worth confirming in-game that players who reached Hostile have enough orc-geas context for it to land; if not, it still reads as defiance even without the full geas understanding.
