# Audit Dialogue Handoff: Auditor's Own Beat (Guardian 3)

The third audit beat. Fires on GuardianRoseEvent where GuardianId = AuditorsOwn. Drops into weighing_audit.yaml in the same {speaker, text} page format as the Warden and Oathkeeper beats. Same length caveat: first-pass, trim in-game once it pages, do not pre-trim.

This is the most distinct voice in the audit, and it required inventing a register from scratch (unlike the Warden, which borrowed the institution, and the Oathkeeper, which borrowed the orcs). Read the voice notes before wiring -- this Guardian breaks several patterns the first two established, on purpose.

## The voice: intimate, not cold

The Auditor's Own judges EXCESS / CRUELTY. Its horror is the inversion of the Under-Warden's: where the bureaucrat is cold indifference, this thing is unbearable INTIMACY. It LIKES cruelty. It reads your excess and is DELIGHTED, because it sees a kindred spirit and wants to connect -- and the connection it offers is unbearable because what it recognizes in you is the worst of you. It is the judge that CONGRATULATES you.

Register on the page:
- **First-person-PLURAL camaraderie.** It says "we," "us," "you and I" where the others accuse. It includes Sasha in itself ("we're the same. We are."). The Under-Warden says "the visiting party"; the Oathkeeper says "you"; the Auditor's Own says "we." That is the ick -- it welcomes you as a monster rather than accusing you of being one.
- **Sibilance carried by sentence-shape**, not heavy phonetic spelling. Long coiling trailing clauses, ellipses that let a thought slither. Light actual s-stretching ("thessse," "ssso") ONLY at peak-intimacy moments. Overdone phonetic hiss reads cartoonish; keep it sparse.
- **Misplaced giggles / wrong-moment delight**, rendered as stage-action in parens or as the sentence breaking into pleasure at exactly the wrong fact.
- **The beast under the membrane.** Underneath the camaraderie is something enormous and starving; the friendliness is a thin skin over appetite, and they are the SAME thing -- it likes you the way a predator likes its meal. The voice flickers: intimacy, a giggle, then for half a beat the floor drops and you feel the size of what's under it.

## The tier INVERSION (critical -- this Guardian scales backwards)

Because it LIKES cruelty, the emotional scaling inverts vs. the other Guardians:
- **Allied** (very low unprovoked-kill rate -- the clean professional): it is DISAPPOINTED. It came up hoping for a friend and found a careful professional who only killed what was necessary. No fun. It loses interest mid-sentence and turns away. This is the clean player's "cold shoulder."
- **Diminished** (low rate): mildly interested but disgusted by your GUILT (the tell that you'd stop if you could).
- **Neutral** (moderate rate -- real excess): warming, courting, "we're going to get along."
- **Savage** (high rate -- the butcher): ECSTATIC, fixated, adoring -- and the adoration IS the threat. It loves you so much it wants to KEEP you.

Emotional inversion lines up with mechanical tier: low investment in you (clean) = low threat; total fixation (butcher) = max threat. So GuardianTier means the same thing mechanically as for the others (Allied = not your enemy / stands aside, Savage = brutal combatant) -- it's only the EMOTIONAL coloring that inverts. At Allied it stands aside out of BOREDOM, not allegiance.

## Composure degrades as adoration rises

The earlier tiers keep composed closing buttons (it's still in control of its performance). ONLY the Savage tier loses its button -- at the peak the friendly performance comes APART, the sentences break down ("I am so -- I am so --"), the beast overrides the words mid-sentence, and the dialogue does not resolve on a clean line; it BREAKS, and the combat begins out of the break. The composure degrading is itself the tier-scaling doing work.

## The Under-Warden's framing shift in this beat

His half here does something his other beats didn't: he WARNS Sasha about the Guardian. "Its interest is not a kindness." "You should be more frightened by that than by anything the others said." For the first time the bureaucrat and Sasha are almost on the same side -- the Under-Warden is unsettled by the thing he has to announce, because it is the thing his whole office exists to keep CONTAINED. This pays off the spectrum idea: the Under-Warden's coldness is the CAGE, and the Auditor's Own is what's in it. The Savage tier's beast-line "there is no office down here" is the beast noting the cage doesn't reach this deep.

## THE AUDITOR'S OWN BEAT -- four tiers (fires on Auditor's Own rise)

### Tier: ALLIED (very low unprovoked-kill rate -- it's disappointed in you)

Under-Warden:
```
The third account concerns excess. Specifically, suffering caused beyond the requirements of descent. This office finds the account nearly empty. You killed what stood against you and little else. What rises to weigh this account is older than this office and does not answer to it; I can only announce it. It has read your record, and I am given to understand it is not pleased with what it found, though not for the reasons the others were.
```
The Auditor's Own (rising, almost immediately losing interest):
```
Oh. Oh, it's... you only killed what you HAD to. Every one of them, a reason. How tidy. How very... necessary. (a small sound, not quite a sigh) I came up out of the warm dark for this? For a careful little man with a careful little ledger of needs-must? No. No, no. You're no fun at all. Go on, then. Go on up the hall. We have nothing to say to each other, you and I. (it is already turning away) Pity. I so hoped you'd be someone.
```

### Tier: DIMINISHED (low rate -- mildly interested, disgusted by your guilt)

Under-Warden:
```
The third account concerns excess. The account is light, but it is not empty. A handful of the dead, here and there, who need not have died. What weighs this account is older than this office. It has found a little to interest it in your record. Only a little. I would not, were I you, give it more.
```
The Auditor's Own (rising, sniffing for the good parts):
```
Mm. There's... a TASTE here. Not much. A few. The ones you didn't strictly need. (a low, pleased hiss, then it pulls back, unsure) I keep looking for the rest of it. The real appetite. It isn't here, is it. You had a few bad afternoons and you felt GUILTY about them. (the warmth curdles slightly) Guilt. I do so dislike guilt. It means you'd stop, if you could. We'll see how much you'd stop. Come and show me.
```

### Tier: NEUTRAL (moderate rate -- real excess; warming, courting)

Under-Warden:
```
The third account concerns excess, and here the account has weight. A pattern of the dead who posed you no threat and received no mercy. This office records it without comment, as is proper. What weighs the account will not be so reserved. It has found a great deal to interest it, and I would remind you that its interest is not a kindness.
```
The Auditor's Own (rising, delighted, leaning in):
```
THERE you are. There you are. I knew there was something under the careful. Look at thessse - (it savors it) - the ones on their knees. The ones running. The one that had already dropped what it was holding. You didn't NEED a single one of them, and you took them anyway, and oh, sweet thing, oh, I FELT that all the way down here. We're going to get along. We're going to get along FAMOUSLY. (a giggle, abrupt, wrong) Don't be shy now. You weren't shy THEN.
```

### Tier: SAVAGE (high rate -- the butcher; it ADORES you, and the performance comes apart)

Under-Warden:
```
The third account concerns excess, and the account is the heaviest this office has ever forwarded to the authority that weighs it. The dead who needed not die fill the file. This office will say only this, and then it will say no more on the matter: what rises now is the thing this office exists to keep contained, and you have spent your descent feeding it. It is very, very pleased with you. You should be more frightened by that than by anything the others said.
```
The Auditor's Own (rising at full, ecstatic, the membrane thinnest -- the friendly mask comes off mid-word; ends by BREAKING, not on a button):
```
THERE. (the word swells, far too large, and the hall feels it) There you are, there you ARE, oh I have WAITED, I have waited so long in the warm dark for one of you to come down to me with hands like THESE - (it is laughing, and the laugh keeps almost becoming something else) - hundreds. HUNDREDS. Who would have let you pass. Who BEGGED. And you, you beautiful thing, you didn't even slow down. You and I, we're the same. We ARE. Say it. (softer, sibilant, unbearably intimate) No - no, you don't have to say it. I can SEE it. It's written all through you, the way it's written all through me. (and here the performance starts to come apart - the friendliness was the cage, and the cage is failing) And I have been so good. So patient. So POLITE, up here where they make me be polite - (the word distorts) - and you, you came down with all that lovely red still on you, and you smell like the warm dark smells, you smell like HOME, and I am so - I am so - (it is no longer making sentences; the beast is at the surface and the friend is gone) - I am so HUNGRY, friend, I have always been so hungry, and you are so CLOSE now, and there is no office down here, there is no office down HERE -
```

Note on the Savage close: it intentionally does NOT resolve on a clean closing line. It ends mid-surge on the trailing dash; the combat begins out of the break. Wiring-wise this is the last page of the savage beat -- the Guardian spawn / fight start should follow immediately from it, so the dialogue "breaks" directly into the encounter. If the paged panel needs a final page to dismiss, the trailing dash IS the dismiss; don't append a resolving line.

## Formatting note for the YAML

The parenthetical stage-actions ((a giggle, abrupt, wrong), (the word distorts), etc.) are part of the delivered text -- they convey the performance that prose can't carry through delivery alone. Render them inline as written (italic if the panel supports it; parenthetical plain text if not). The CAPS on certain words mark stress/swell; render as written or as emphasis styling if the panel supports it. If the panel can't do italic or emphasis, the plain text reads fine -- the word choice and rhythm carry it.
