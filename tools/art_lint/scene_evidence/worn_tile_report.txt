Worn-tile (floor_worn, 3001) in-frame check — docs/art_test_scene_spec_v2.md §5 merge evidence.
v3 repack re-run (supersedes the PR #8 baseline report).

Method: Main.LogWornTilePositions() calls FloorComposer.Compose(state.Map, seed: 0) — the
exact same inputs DungeonRenderer.Render used internally (seed: 0 is Render's own default
parameter, not reproduced from a guess) — to recover which cells it marked Worn, a result
Render computes but never returns to the caller. Cells suppressed back to Standard under a
blocking prop footprint (DungeonRenderer's own suppression rule) are excluded, so a
suppressed cell is not misreported as "worn and in frame" when it visually isn't. The
in-frame check intersects against the actual visible tile rect, computed by inverting
PlayerCamera's own position/scale math (ComputeVisibleTileRect) — not assumed from the
room's authored bounds.

FINDING during the repack: the first repacked layout (8x9 interior, player (5,6), before
any position adjustment) rendered ZERO floor_worn tiles at all — not just zero in frame,
zero in the entire room. Root cause, confirmed by live diagnostic capture: MarkPropCell
(called for every blocking prop, matching production RoomPropPlacer behavior) makes
prop-occupied cells act as pseudo-walls for FloorComposer's edge-darkening pass. In this
densely-packed room that left only 10 truly Standard-eligible cells, and none of their
noise values (max observed: 0.5517) crossed the 0.72 worn threshold. This was verified
against the real SimplexNoise implementation (src/Logic/Map/SimplexNoise.cs, no Godot
dependency) over the actual 10 eligible cells, not a hypothetical reconstruction — a
translation search over those exact cells found (dx=0, dy=+1) raises the max noise to
0.8351 while preserving the room's shape and every relative adjacency. The final builder
applies this shift: same content, translated one tile, seed and renderer untouched.
